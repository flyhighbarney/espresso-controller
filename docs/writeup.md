# What I Would Propose Exploring on the Luxe Cafe Platform

The core insight from this project is that **estimating puck hydraulic resistance in real time** — rather than treating it as an unmeasured disturbance — unlocks meaningfully better shot consistency at retail-price sensor budgets. A pressure transducer and a flow meter (total < $25 in volume) give the Kalman filter enough information to track resistance as the puck compacts and channels during extraction. The adaptive controller then schedules its gains against this estimate instead of relying on fixed tuning that can only be optimal for one puck state.

On the Luxe Cafe platform, I would propose integrating this estimator into the existing control firmware as a software-only upgrade — no additional sensors beyond what the machine already uses for pressure safety and dose metering. The TinyML predictor adds a second layer: by learning each user's grind/dose signature over their first few shots, the machine can pre-tune itself shot-to-shot without manual profiling. This is the kind of adaptive behavior that differentiates a connected appliance from a fixed-program one, and it fits the Ninja brand story of "professional results without professional skill." The estimated puck resistance could also feed a user-facing freshness or grind-quality indicator, turning an internal control signal into a consumer feature.
